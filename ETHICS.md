# Responsible use

Unseen is a live-conversation assistant. Like any tool that transcribes people and
hides itself from screen capture, it can be used well or badly. We'd rather state
the line than pretend it doesn't exist.

## What Unseen is for

- Taking notes and tracking action items in your own meetings.
- Having your product docs, prices, or prep notes at hand while you talk.
- Practicing interviews and presentations with a coach in the corner.
- Following a lecture in a field you're new to.

## What it is not for

- **Deceiving a counterparty who is evaluating you.** Using Unseen to feed answers
  in a real job interview, certification exam, or proctored assessment
  misrepresents your abilities to someone entitled to assess them. The
  interview profile exists for *practice*.
- **Covert recording.** Many jurisdictions require all-party consent to record or
  transcribe a conversation. You are responsible for getting it. See
  [docs/privacy-and-consent.md](docs/privacy-and-consent.md).

## Why Privacy Mode exists

Privacy Mode keeps the overlay out of *your own* screen share — the same reason
presenter notes don't show on the projector. That's the legitimate 99% case, and
it's why the toggle is visible in the header rather than buried: the feature is
for hiding your notes, not for hiding the fact that you're using notes from
people who have a right to know.

## Data

Everything stays local: transcripts, answers, settings. Keys live in your OS
keychain. There is no telemetry of any kind.
